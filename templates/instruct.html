HondaBox

Instructions

You can download your pictures to your car's infotainment system via one of two ways:

Method 1: In-car browser

The easiest way is to just download the pictures directly from the internet onto your car's system. After uploading your picture you'll be given a link where your background pictures can be downloaded directly from the car from the in-car web browser.

Some Hondas come from the factory with a browser enabled. On more recent models, Honda has disabled the browser. If you don't have access to a web browser (or don't know what I'm talking about at all here) follow the instructions in the first video below to bring it back. Of course, you'll also need internet access in the car. Surprisingly, your car may already have access to the internet, via HondaLink. You can try just enabling the browser (via the first video) and seeing if you have a connection. If you don't, then follow the instructions in the third video to connect to WiFi.

  • YouTube: Enable browser and links in Honda
  • YouTube: Honda Box tutorial
  • YouTube: Connect Honda to WiFi

I know this is a lot of setup, but once you've done it once, you never have to do it again! You can create a bookmark on one of your home screens, and from then on you don't have to go through any of the setup and will have a really easy and quick way to move media from your phone or computer to your car from then on.

Method 2: USB stick

Most Hondas with allow the user upload background pictures via a USB stick. You can always download the processed images from this website to your computer and then copy to a USB stick. Consult your car's user manual for instructions on how to upload pictures from the USB stick to your car. Let me know if you need help on this and I'll be happy to find a video (or make one) showing how to do so.
